# Selected Projects
A curated selection of projects demonstrating how data science, AI-enabled tools and systems thinking can be applied to practical operational problems.

The emphasis is on useful, credible work shaped by real workflows, organisational constraints, stakeholder needs and decision-making contexts. These projects vary in scope and method, but each demonstrates some combination of problem framing, data preparation, analytical design, interactive tooling, modelling, automation or operational decision support.

The aim is not to present isolated code samples. It is to show how analytical and AI-enabled approaches can be embedded into systems that people can use, trust and understand.

## School Allocation Tool
<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/pierswalker71/datascience_experiments/main/schools/images/school_allocation_map.png"
     alt="Map preview of the school allocation tool"
     width="320">
     
An interactive app designed to help local authorities create fair, geographically coherent and defensible school allocation groups.

The tool combines school-level data with official location information and generates proposed groups that balance school counts, pupil numbers and geographic practicality. Users can adjust assumptions, review summary metrics and inspect the proposed allocation on an interactive map.

It is designed as a decision-support aid to give officers a useful starting point for scenario planning, discussion and refinement.

**Domain:** Local government, education  
**Problem type:** Operational allocation, decision support  
**Users:** Local authority officers, service managers, education teams  
**Methods:** Rule-based sorting and distribution, geospatial review, scenario testing  

**Tags:** `Decision support` `Local Authority` `Operational planning` `Marimo app`

## Simulation Optimisation

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/pierswalker71/Simulation_Optimisation/main/images/simulator_optimisation_output_scatter.JPG"
  alt="Scatter plot of simulation optimisation results showing performance and cost"
  width="320">

A prototype decision-support workflow showing how simulation, machine learning and optimisation can be combined to identify low-cost ways of achieving a target operational outcome.

The project treats a simulator as a black box, generates data from a range of input combinations, trains a surrogate regression model to approximate the simulator’s behaviour, and then uses optimisation to search for input settings that meet a target output at minimum cost.

It demonstrates how an organisation could move beyond manually testing individual scenarios towards a more systematic approach for resource planning, cost reduction and operational decision-making.

**Domain:** Operations, resource planning, simulation modelling
**Problem type:** Simulation optimisation, cost-aware decision support
**Users:** Analysts, operational planners, service managers, decision-makers
**Methods:** Simulation, surrogate modelling, regression, global optimisation, scenario testing

**Tags:** `Decision support` `Simulation optimisation` `Surrogate modelling` `Operational planning` `Python`
