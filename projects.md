# Selected Projects
A curated selection of projects demonstrating how data science, AI-enabled tools and systems thinking can be applied to practical operational problems.

The emphasis is on useful, credible work shaped by real workflows, organisational constraints, stakeholder needs and decision-making contexts. These projects vary in scope and method, but each demonstrates some combination of problem framing, data preparation, analytical design, interactive tooling, modelling, automation or operational decision support.

The aim is not to present isolated code samples. It is to show how analytical and AI-enabled approaches can be embedded into systems that people can use, trust and understand.

## School Allocation Tool
<img src="[/images/school-allocation-preview.pn](https://github.com/pierswalker71/datascience_experiments/blob/main/schools/images/school_allocation_map.png)g"
     alt="Map preview of the school allocation tool"
     width="420">
     
An interactive app designed to help local authorities create fair, geographically coherent and defensible school allocation groups.

The tool combines school-level data with official location information and generates proposed groups that balance school counts, pupil numbers and geographic practicality. Users can adjust assumptions, review summary metrics and inspect the proposed allocation on an interactive map.

It is deliberately designed as a transparent decision-support tool rather than a black-box model. The aim is to give officers and managers a useful starting point for scenario planning, discussion and refinement.

**Domain:** Local government, education  
**Problem type:** Operational allocation, decision support  
**Users:** Local authority officers, service managers, education teams  
**Methods:** Rule-based sorting and distribution, geospatial review, scenario testing  

**Tags:** `Decision support` `Local Authority` `Operational planning` `Marimo app`
